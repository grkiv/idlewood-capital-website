Proposed Equity Recapitalization

A strategic opportunity at the five-year milestone

Dear Investors,

Alongside the attached Q3 2025 Investor Update, we are presenting a proposal for the next phase of this investment: an equity recapitalization of Monroe Road Investors, LLC. The original business plan anticipated a five-year hold period, and that milestone will be reached next month. Over this time, the property has been fully repositioned, with more than $1.2 million of improvements, strong new leases, and market rent growth that reinforces its long-term value.

This recapitalization is designed with three objectives. First, it provides each investor with the option to receive a cash distribution economically equivalent to a sale at today's independently appraised value of $5.72 million. Second, it allows investors who wish to remain invested to continue participating in future cash flow and appreciation on a newly established equity base. Third, it refines the ongoing fee structure to ensure continued alignment between the general partners and investors and to support active long-term management of the property.

The sections that follow outline why this recapitalization makes strategic sense at this point in the investment, the current property and market fundamentals, the valuation basis, the proposed capital structure, and the specific options available to each investor.

Why a Recapitalization Now

Strategic timing and compelling fundamentals

This is an ideal moment to recapitalize the Monroe Road investment. Several factors make the timing and structure compelling:

  • Five-year milestone - The original investment plan anticipated a five-year hold, and that horizon is now reached. We have executed the business plan ahead of expectations and created durable value.
  • Value creation complete - More than $1.2 million has been invested in two major construction phases, resulting in a fully repositioned property. New long-term leases with Apotheca and Angry Ales, together with a stable 4732 tenant mix, position the property for strong cash flow and embedded rent growth.
  • Market strength - Charlotte's retail fundamentals remain exceptional. Low vacancy, limited new supply, and rising rents (particularly in the Oakhurst/Monroe Road corridor) support continued appreciation and provide upside when existing below-market leases roll.
  • Favorable financing window - We have secured financing with a blended interest rate of roughly 4.9 percent that remains fixed through December 2028. This cost of capital is well below current market rates and provides a multi-year window of rate certainty to support cash flow and growth.
  • Modernized structure and enhanced sponsor commitment - The property is fundamentally different from when we acquired it, and the proposed financing structure requires significantly more personal guarantees from the sponsors than originally anticipated. Total project debt will reach $3.34 million compared to the initial plan of $1.25 million, representing substantially increased personal risk and liability for the general partners. A recapitalization allows us to refine the fee structure to reflect both current market standards and the present scale and complexity of the asset, while maintaining clear alignment between the general partners and investors for the next stage of ownership.

Taken together, these factors make a recapitalization the most strategic way to capture the value created to date, provide liquidity to those who want it, and position the partnership for continued success in the years ahead.

Property & Market Highlights

Five years of transformation and value creation

The Monroe Road property today is stronger and more valuable than at any point in its history. Over the past five years we have transformed the asset through targeted investment and active management:

  • Capital improvements - More than $1.2 million invested across two major construction phases, including a full renovation of the 4726 building and substantial upgrades to the 4732 building. Both now feature new roofs, modern HVAC and electrical systems, improved façades, and other infrastructure enhancements.
  • Leasing strength - The property is anchored by a stable core of tenants in the 4732 building (Singas Pizza, The Photo Outfitters, and Lemonade Fitness), operating on triple-net leases with annual rent increases. New long-term leases with Apotheca and Angry Ales further diversify and strengthen cash flow, with Apotheca commencing rent in October 2025 and Angry Ales expected to open in early spring 2026. Several of the 4732 leases are now below current market rents, providing built-in upside when they renew or roll.
  • Market momentum - Charlotte's retail fundamentals remain exceptionally strong. Vacancy rates are near historic lows, new construction is limited, and the Oakhurst/Monroe Road corridor continues to benefit from robust population and income growth. Asking rents for comparable space have risen significantly in the past two years, supporting both near-term income and long-term appreciation.

These improvements and market dynamics provide a solid foundation for the proposed recapitalization. They also reinforce that the property is well positioned for stable cash flow today and for meaningful rent growth and value creation in the years ahead.

Proposed Capital Structure

Strategic leverage with below-market fixed-rate debt

The recapitalization is structured to keep the property's balance sheet strong while providing significant cash to investors and a clear equity base for the next stage of ownership. It combines the current first-lien mortgage with a second-lien loan scheduled to close this month:

Loan Amount Rate Terms Maturity
First-lien loan $1,694,395 3.45% 25-year amortization Dec 2028
Second-lien loan $1,650,000 6.50% 25-year amort. (12 mo. I/O) Dec 2028
Total Debt $3,344,395 ~4.9% blended 25 years Dec 2028

After funding and paying off interim obligations, total property debt will stand at roughly $3,344,395, resulting in a loan-to-value ratio of about 58% based on the $5.72 million appraisal.

Proceeds from the new loan will be applied as follows:

  • Retire approximately $442,571 of accounts payable (including construction payables and leasing commissions)
  • Fund a preferred return catch-up of $45,600
  • Return the entire $570,000 initial investment to investors
  • Pay a 2% recapitalization fee to the sponsors
  • Cover closing costs of roughly $23,250 and fund a working-capital reserve of $25,000

These uses of funds are cash distributions and operating reserves funded from new debt. They do not reduce the property's equity base, which remains the difference between the appraised value and total post-recap debt. After the recapitalization the partnership would own the property with an equity value of approximately $2,375,605 ($5.72 million appraised value less $3.34 million total debt).

As part of this proposal, the existing promote structure would be converted into an equity position, with 35% of the total equity allocated to the general partners and 65% (about $1.54 million) remaining with the limited partners. This conversion ensures the general partners' interests are directly tied to the ongoing ownership and long-term performance of the property, while establishing a clear equity base for calculating each investor's continuing ownership and future distributions.

* All amounts shown are estimates based on current projections and are subject to adjustment upon finalization of loan closing costs and other transaction expenses.

Investor Options

Flexible choices tailored to individual investment objectives

This recapitalization is designed to let each investor tailor their participation to individual objectives. At closing, investors will be able to choose among the following paths:

Option A – Cash Out (Sale-Equivalent Liquidity)

Elect to receive a cash distribution economically equivalent to what a sale today would provide, based on the $5.72 million appraisal and the current partnership waterfall. This payout includes full return of capital, all accrued preferred return, and each investor's pro-rata share of distributable profits. For example, a 50,000-share investor would receive an estimated total of approximately $203,627.*

* Subject to final closing adjustments and finalization of all transaction costs.

Option B – Remain Invested

Elect to continue in the investment and participate in future cash flow and appreciation. Investors choosing this option will receive a partial distribution at closing (consisting of their accrued preferred return, the full return of their original capital, and their share of current distributable profits) while rolling their remaining ownership into the recapitalized entity. The rolled amount will represent each investor's share of the limited partners' equity base of about $1.54 million, benefiting from the new capital structure and ongoing market upside.

Option C – Increase Investment Level

Subject to availability and final documentation, investors may request to increase their investment beyond their current pro-rata share. Allocation priority for any additional equity will be given to the sponsors and existing investors before any potential outside capital is considered.

Option D – Partial Cash Out

Elect to reduce your ongoing investment while maintaining a smaller ownership position in the recapitalized entity. This option allows you to receive a partial cash distribution while continuing to participate in future cash flow and appreciation at a reduced level that fits your investment objectives.

Each option is designed to provide flexibility while maintaining fairness and transparency. All elections will be reflected in the final recapitalization agreements, which will proceed only upon investor approval.

Post-Recap Partnership Structure

Simplified waterfall with clear alignment

Equity & Contributions

  • Total equity after recapitalization will be approximately $2.38 million (based on a $5.72 million appraisal and $3.34 million total debt)
  • All equity will be treated as Limited Partner equity on a pari passu basis
  • The general partners will participate in the equity alongside all other investors
  • The existing 35% promote structure will be retained for distributions beyond the preferred return and capital recovery

Preferred Return

8.0% annual, non-compounding preferred return will be paid on all equity, pari passu.

Cash Flow & Capital Event Waterfalls

After operating expenses and debt service, distributions will follow this sequence:

  1. Preferred Return to All Equity - 8% annual on all invested capital, paid current and catch-up if necessary, pari passu
  2. Return of All Capital - 100% of all invested capital, pari passu
  3. Promoted Distributions - All remaining cash flow and sale or refinance proceeds will be distributed 35% to the General Partners and 65% to all Limited Partners pro rata based on their equity ownership

This updated framework simplifies the structure while preserving key protections (preferred return and full capital recovery) yet clearly defines how the former promote is converted into a permanent equity interest. It keeps the partnership aligned and transparent as the property enters its next phase of income and appreciation.
