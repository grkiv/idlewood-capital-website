Five-Year Anniversary Update

Executive Summary

Five years of growth and strategic repositioning

Dear Investors,

Next month marks the fifth anniversary of our investment in the Monroe Road property. Over these five years, the project has grown into something larger and stronger than we originally envisioned, exceeding our expectations in construction quality, tenant strength, and overall operations. The property today is more resilient and better positioned for long-term growth than when we began.

This letter provides a complete update on the property's current status and our plan for the coming years. It covers construction progress, leasing and operations, market conditions, financing, and the strategic path forward.

Construction Progress

Two-phase renovation program transforms property beyond original condition

The first phase of construction, completed in 2022, invested approximately $800,000 into improvements to the 4732 building. These upgrades stabilized the property and enhanced its overall appeal.

The second phase, a full renovation of the 4726 building, has now been completed. Last week we turned the space over to our new tenant, Apotheca, marking a major milestone in the property's repositioning. This $433,000 project included a new roof, all-new HVAC and electrical systems, new gas service to the building, new bathrooms, and façade improvements to match the 4732 building. The result is a fully modernized asset ready for long-term tenancy.

Together these two phases, representing roughly $1.2 million of total investment, have transformed the property well beyond its original condition and positioned it to capture higher rents and stronger tenant demand for years to come.

Two new long-term leases materially enhance the property's stability and growth profile. Apotheca is finalizing its buildout in the newly renovated 4726 building and will begin paying rent in October. Angry Ales has signed a new lease for the 4800 building, is investing in substantial improvements, and is expected to open in early spring 2026.

The 4732 building remains exceptionally stable, with Singas Pizza, The Photo Outfitters, and Lemonade Fitness all operating under strong triple-net leases with minimal near-term rollover risk. Because these leases were signed before the rapid escalation of market rents in Charlotte and in the Oakhurst submarket, their current rates are below today's market levels, creating future upside when these leases roll and are renewed or re-leased.

This combination of high-quality new leases, a solid core of strong but below-market leases in 4732, and only a small vacancy positions the property for durable cash flow today and significant rent growth potential in the years ahead.

Market Context

Charlotte retail fundamentals remain exceptionally strong with favorable conditions for future growth

Charlotte's retail fundamentals remain exceptionally strong. Vacancy rates are near historic lows, and tenant demand continues to grow, driven by sustained population and income growth. New retail construction in the city and in the Oakhurst/Monroe Road corridor remains limited, which is helping drive rents higher across well-located neighborhood centers.

Within our immediate trade area, asking rents have risen sharply over the past two years. New leases for well-positioned retail space are now commonly being signed at rates materially higher than those in place when most of the 4732 leases were executed. This dynamic explains why several of our existing tenants are paying rents below today's market levels.

These conditions give us confidence in the property's long-term value. The market's limited supply pipeline, ongoing household growth, and rising lease rates provide a favorable backdrop for future rent growth and further appreciation, supporting both current operations and the strategic financing initiatives we are planning.

Financing

Strategic debt structure locks in low-cost capital and provides flexibility for growth

The property is well positioned with long-term, low-cost financing. Our current first-lien loan has a balance of $1,694,000, carries a 3.45% fixed interest rate, amortizes over 25 years, and matures in December 2028. This stable, low-rate debt provides excellent cash flow coverage and a strong foundation for the asset.

We plan to close this month on a second-lien loan of $1.65 million with First Bank. Key terms include a fixed 6.50% interest rate, a term running alongside the first lien to December 2028, and a 25-year amortization with the first 12 months interest-only. Proceeds of this new loan will fund the recent construction and provide distributable cash that gives us flexibility for a planned equity recapitalization.

That provides a significant advantage over the next three years by keeping debt service low, preserving cash flow, and giving us flexibility to execute the next phase of the property's business plan, including the contemplated equity recapitalization.

Next Steps

Opportune timing for equity recapitalization with independent appraisal supporting strategic repositioning

The original business plan anticipated a five-year hold period, which we reach next month. With construction complete, leasing momentum strong, and long-term financing secured at a blended interest rate far below current market levels, this is an opportune time to take the next step.

We are preparing an equity recapitalization that we will present to investors for consideration. The proposal will give each investor the choice of liquidity through a partial or full cash-out or the opportunity to remain in the investment and participate in future cash flow and appreciation. As part of this process, we recently obtained an independent appraisal from Integra Realty Resources dated September 2, 2025, which concluded an as-is market value of approximately $5.72 million. This value will serve as the basis for the upcoming recapitalization proposal. The recapitalization will also refine the ongoing fee structure to ensure strong alignment between the general partners and investors and to support active, long-term management of the property.

A separate document detailing the proposed recapitalization—including valuation, structure, and projected investor economics—will follow shortly alongside this update.
